## Description (en)

Paragraph 175, known formally as §175 StGB and also referred to as Section 175 in English, was a provision of the German Criminal Code adopted after the unification of Germany in 1871. It criminalized sexual acts between men - discussions about including sexual acts between women never made it to actual legislation. The provision served as a main target for the early German sexual rights movements, with petitions for its repeal being circulated from 1897 onwards. A repeal was attempted in 1929, but the unstable political situation, with uncertain parliamentary majorities and the rise of the Nazi Party, prevented implementation of the repeal. Nazi expansion of Paragraph 175 resulted in those convicted under the provision often being put in concentration camps. After WWII, the statute was used to keep those convicted imprisoned and denied reparations. East Germany abolished Paragraph 175 in 1968, after having made it functionally void in 1958. West Germany retained the expanded Nazi version, but after successive revisions only cases involving persons under the age of 18 were prosecuted after 1973. The section was eliminated during the reconciliation of East and West German legal codes in 1994.
