## Description (en)

Term used to describe the ways in which disabled and chronically ill people experience and relate to time differently than is socially expected. According to Alison Kafer, quoted in a paper published by Ellen Samuels, 'rather than bend disabled bodies and minds to meet the clock, crip time bends the clock to meet disabled bodies and minds.'
