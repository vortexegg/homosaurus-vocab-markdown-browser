## Description (en)

People with complete gonadal dysgenesis are born with XX or XY chromosomes and gonadal tissue that has not developed into testicles or ovaries (also known as streak gonads). They are generally born with a vulva and vagina, and they will not usually develop secondary sex characteristics at puberty because streak gonads do not produce hormones.
