## Description (en)

People with Fraser syndrome are usually born with differences in the development of their eyelids, fingers, and toes. They may also have variations in their sex characteristics, such as a larger-than-typical clitoris or undescended testes. They may develop without one or both kidneys, and other parts of the urinary system can additionally be affected.
