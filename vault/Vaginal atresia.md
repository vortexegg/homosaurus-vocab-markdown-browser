## Description (en)

People with this variation may be born without a vaginal opening, a shallow “dimple” where an opening would typically be, or a vaginal opening that is fused or blocked by fibrous tissue. People with vaginal atresia often have a typical upper vaginal canal (along with a typical uterus, cervix, fallopian tubes, and ovaries) with differences in the lower portion of the vagina only.
